coffee-archive-copilot

AI-assisted archive and interpretation system for coffee experiences.

coffee-archive-copilot helps capture coffee experiences as they happen — bean cards, cup photos, café observations, tasting notes, places, rituals, and memories — and organize them into a searchable archive built for interpretation over time.

The project is based on a simple idea:

Archive is infrastructure. Interpretation is the product.

The goal is not to replace personal interpretation, but to preserve, structure, and enrich it.

AI suggests. The user decides.

What This App Does

* captures coffee experiences in a structured archive
* preserves context around cups, places, and memories
* organizes entries through themes, flags, and collections
* helps surface patterns across taste, place, ritual, and creative interest
* supports creative interpretation of coffee experiences through collections, themes, and visual directions

Product Philosophy

A coffee entry is not only a tasting record.

It can also capture:

* where the coffee was experienced
* what mood or memory surrounded it
* what kind of attention it opened up
* how it connects to other cups over time
* whether it belongs to a larger story or collection

The archive exists so interpretation can happen.

Core Workflow

Capture → Archive → Interpret → Publish → Learn

Long-Term Direction

The long-term goal is to turn scattered coffee experiences into a creative archive that can answer questions such as:

* What cups do I remember most?
* What places repeatedly appear in my archive?
* What themes emerge across cities and trips?
* What kinds of coffee experiences inspire writing?
* How has my taste changed over time?
* What kinds of content resonate with readers?

The ultimate output is not a coffee database.

It is a richer understanding of coffee, memory, place, and creative practice.
